About

I am an Interdisciplinary Honors student in engineering at the University of Washington, Seattle. My work spans the bench and the clinic. I run molecular biology experiments as an undergraduate research assistant at Fred Hutch, help design diagnostic hardware for low-resource healthcare settings with Bioengineers Without Borders, and contribute to protein engineering and public engagement work with Washington iGEM.

Before college I published independent research on polygenic risk scores and machine learning in healthcare, interned in a bioengineering lab at Seattle Children’s Hospital, and led international teams delivering medical supplies and advocating against algorithmic bias. The through-line is the same in all of it, making the tools of modern biology accurate and available to the people who are usually left out of them.
